The Origins of Israelite Monotheism

How God's revelation took shape in history

The Core Question

How did ancient Israel develop an exclusive monotheistic faith in a polytheistic ancient Near East? We will explore this through two distinct, yet complementary scholarly paradigms.

Paradigm 1

Evolution & Convergence

Mark S. Smith, The Early History of God

🤝 Convergence

Yahweh absorbed the positive attributes of El (Creator) and Baal (Storm God/Warrior).

✂️ Differentiation

Later prophets rigorously rejected Canaanite roots (like Asherah) to establish strict monotheism.

📖 Read Jiwon's Full Script & Evidence

El (Canaanite High God): We can see that El was the original god just by looking at their name: "Isra-el", not "Isra-yahweh". Genesis 49:24-25 identifies Yahweh with "El Shadday".

Baal (Storm God): Israelite kings needed a powerful national warrior-god. Psalm 29 (The "Canaanite Psalm") describes Yahweh using language originally dedicated to Baal.

Asherah: The Kuntillet Ajrud inscriptions contain a blessing: "I bless you by Yahweh of Samaria and his Asherah."

"According to Smith, ancient Israel did not begin as strictly monotheistic. Instead, its religion developed over time through a process called 'Convergence'... Israelite monotheism was not a sudden event, but a gradual process of Convergence and Differentiation."

Paradigm 2

Crisis & Trauma in Exile

David M. Carr, Holy Resilience

🔥 The Crucible

The destruction of Jerusalem (586 BCE) forced a massive theological leap to survive the trauma.

⚖️ Theodicy

Dtr2 & Ezekiel framed the disaster not as Yahweh's weakness, but as His sovereign judgment.

📖 Read Cosmos's Full Script & Evidence

Dtr2 & Ezekiel: Taking responsibility for the trauma gave the exiles a sense of control. If disaster was punishment for disobedience, returning to Yahweh could bring restoration.

Second Isaiah: Made the radical leap that Yahweh is the ONLY God in the universe. He even called the Persian King Cyrus Yahweh's "Messiah" (anointed one).

Isaiah 45:5: "I am the LORD, and there is no other; besides me there is no god."

"According to Carr, the true crucible of pure monotheism was the Babylonian Exile... They entered the exile worshipping a national god, but they survived it by worshipping the Universal Sovereign."

A Complementary Synthesis

These two models are not contradictory. They are highly complementary.

  • Mark S. Smith provides the Ingredients (Raw Materials): Cultural evolution provided the theological framework.
  • David M. Carr provides the Crucible (Fire): The trauma of Exile was the ultimate catalyst that forged those materials into absolute monotheism.
📖 Read Yeseob's Conclusion

"Both models show us that Israel's monotheism was forged in the messy and painful reality of human history. This act of 'Holy Resilience' became the survival mechanism that allowed Judaism and Christianity to endure historical traumas."

As Dr. Botta has reminded us, understanding this history doesn't threaten us; it helps us bring a deeper, more grounded faith into our own communities today.

Questions for Discussion

Q2
