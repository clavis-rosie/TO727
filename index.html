The Origins of Israelite Monotheism

How God's revelation took shape in history

Jiwon • Cosmos • Yeseob

The Core Question

How did ancient Israel develop an exclusive monotheistic faith in a polytheistic ancient Near East? We explore this through two distinct, yet complementary paradigms.

Paradigm 1

Evolution & Convergence

Based on: Mark S. Smith, The Early History of God

  • Convergence: Yahweh gradually absorbed the attributes of El (Creator, Father) and Baal (Storm God, Warrior).
  • Evidence: Genesis 49, Psalm 29 (The "Canaanite Psalm"), and the Kuntillet Ajrud inscriptions ("Yahweh and his Asherah").
  • Differentiation: Later prophets vigorously rejected Canaanite roots to establish strict monotheism.

Paradigm 2

Crisis & Trauma

Based on: David M. Carr, Holy Resilience

  • The Crucible of Exile (586 BCE): The destruction of Jerusalem posed a theological crisis. Did Marduk defeat Yahweh?
  • Theodicy & Dtr2: Exilic editors reframed the disaster as God's sovereign punishment for breaking the covenant.
  • Absolute Monotheism: Second Isaiah made the radical leap: Yahweh is the universal sovereign, and other gods do not exist.

Synthesis: A Complementary View

These models are not contradictory. Mark Smith provides the "Raw Materials" (cultural evolution and theological framework), while David Carr provides the "Crucible" (the catastrophic trauma that forced those materials into strict monotheism).

"Holy Resilience became the survival mechanism that allowed faith to endure historical traumas."

Discussion Questions

1. Does the historical reality that faith evolved through "messy" cultural convergence and crisis threaten biblical authority, or does it beautifully illustrate an 'incarnational' revelation?

2. Between cultural convergence and historical crisis, which model do you find more compelling, and why?
